# AI Readiness Brief: Randeep Bhatia

**Role:** public-sector procurement lead

## Where I stand
I am a public-sector procurement lead with twelve years of experience in vendor evaluation, RFP drafting, and compliance management across three jurisdictions. My LENS audit revealed that approximately two-thirds of my AI-related professional anxiety is manufactured — driven by technical terminology that sits below my Level Threshold and does not affect any decision I make. My one real gap is practical: I have not yet used AI tools for the document drafting tasks I perform every week, despite clear evidence that this is feasible and relevant. My Minimum Viable Knowledge Threshold focuses on output evaluation, workflow integration, and compliance risk — not model architecture or engineering. My 90-day target is to produce first-draft RFP scope sections using AI tools, reducing drafting time while maintaining the compliance judgment that defines my professional value. My first action is a 45-minute session this week using a general-purpose AI tool on a real RFP. The single most important thing I am releasing is the obligation to understand how AI systems work at a technical level — that knowledge belongs to someone else's job.

## My Level Threshold
**Above:** Output evaluation (can I trust this AI-generated clause or cost estimate?), workflow integration (where does AI fit in the RFP and evaluation process?), vendor claim assessment (is this AI capability real and relevant to my procurement context?), risk and compliance awareness (what are the failure modes that matter in public-sector contracting?).
**Below:** Model architecture and training methods, embedding and vector database infrastructure, fine-tuning and prompt engineering at the engineering level, AI system deployment and maintenance, transformer internals.

## My Edge
I bring twelve years of public-sector procurement law and vendor evaluation experience combined with the ability to use AI to draft and compare contract clauses and RFP scope sections at speed — while keeping the compliance judgment and supplier relationship context that no model can replicate.

## First action (next 7 days)
Within the next 7 days, I will spend 45 minutes using a general-purpose AI tool to draft the scope section of the next RFP on my desk — the facilities management renewal due in three weeks. I will give the tool a structured brief (objectives, constraints, evaluation criteria) and produce a first draft I can redline. I will know I have done it when I have a redlined draft saved in my working folder. Skill this builds: directing an AI tool to produce a domain-specific document draft.
